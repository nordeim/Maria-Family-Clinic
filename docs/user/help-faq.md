## General Platform Questions

### What is My Family Clinic?
My Family Clinic is Singapore's comprehensive healthcare platform that helps you find family doctors, book appointments, and manage your health journey. We're integrated with Singapore's Healthier SG program to make preventive healthcare accessible to all residents.

### Who can use My Family Clinic?
- Singapore Citizens and Permanent Residents
- Visitors can browse clinic information but need local registration for appointments
- All age groups welcome, with special focus on Healthier SG (ages 40+)
- Healthcare providers can access administrative features

### Is My Family Clinic free to use?
Yes! Basic platform features are completely free:
- Doctor and clinic search
- Appointment booking
- Health information viewing
- Basic health plan tracking

Some services may involve standard healthcare consultation fees charged by clinics, but our platform doesn't add any booking fees.

### How is My Family Clinic different from other healthcare platforms?
- **Healthier SG Integration**: Seamless integration with Singapore's national preventive healthcare program
- **Government Compliance**: Full adherence to MOH standards and regulations
- **Multilingual Support**: Complete support for English, Mandarin, Malay, and Tamil
- **Accessibility Features**: WCAG 2.2 AA compliant for users with disabilities
- **Comprehensive Care**: Focus on preventive care, not just treatment

## Account & Registration

### How do I create an account?
1. Visit our website or download our mobile app
2. Click "Sign Up" 
3. Choose registration method:
   - Email and password
   - SingPass (recommended for Healthier SG)
   - Singapore phone number
4. Complete verification (SMS/email)
5. Set up your profile with basic health information

### Do I need SingPass?
SingPass is recommended but not required. SingPass provides:
- Faster registration process
- Automatic eligibility checking for Healthier SG
- Secure access to your health records
- Integration with HealthHub

Without SingPass, you'll need to manually verify your eligibility for Healthier SG and may have limited access to some features.

### Can I register without a Singapore phone number?
Yes, but you'll need a Singapore phone number for:
- SMS verification
- Appointment reminders
- Two-factor authentication
- Important health notifications

Consider getting a local SIM card if you're staying in Singapore long-term.

### What if I forget my password?
1. Click "Forgot Password" on the login page
2. Enter your registered email address
3. Check your email for reset instructions
4. Follow the link to create a new password
5. If you don't receive the email, check spam folder or contact support

### How do I update my personal information?
1. Log in to your account
2. Go to "Profile Settings" or "Account Settings"
3. Click "Edit Profile"
4. Update your information (name, contact details, emergency contacts)
5. Save changes
6. Verify important changes (phone, email) via SMS/email confirmation

### Can I have multiple profiles on one account?
Yes, you can manage health information for family members:
- Add family members as authorized users
- Book appointments on their behalf
- Access their health information (with consent)
- Manage multiple Healthier SG enrollments
- Set different notification preferences for each person

### How do I delete my account?
1. Log in to your account
2. Go to "Privacy & Security" settings
3. Click "Delete Account"
4. Review the information about data retention
5. Confirm account deletion
6. You'll receive confirmation email

**Note**: Healthcare records are retained for 6 years as required by law, even after account deletion.

## Healthier SG Program

### What is the Healthier SG program?
Healthier SG is Singapore's national preventive healthcare program launched by MOH in 2023. It helps residents aged 40+ take proactive steps to manage their health through personalized health plans with family doctors.

**Key Benefits:**
- Access to a family doctor
- Personalized health plans
- Subsidized health screenings
- Vaccinations at reduced costs
- Chronic disease management support

### Am I eligible for Healthier SG?
**Eligibility Requirements:**
- Age: 40 years or older
- Status: Singapore Citizen or Permanent Resident
- No minimum health requirements

**Priority Groups (for faster enrollment):**
- Ages 60 and above
- People with chronic diseases (diabetes, hypertension, high cholesterol)
- High-risk individuals

### How do I enroll in Healthier SG?
1. **Check Eligibility**: Use our eligibility checker
2. **Choose Doctor**: Find a participating doctor through our platform
3. **HealthHub Registration**: Register through the HealthHub app/website using SingPass
4. **Select Clinic**: Choose your preferred participating clinic
5. **Book First Consultation**: Schedule within 3 months of enrollment

### Can I enroll without receiving an SMS invitation?
Yes! While MOH sends SMS invitations, you can self-enroll without an invitation. All eligible residents can enroll directly through HealthHub.

### What if I don't want to enroll immediately?
Enrollment is completely voluntary. You can:
- Decline the SMS invitation
- Self-register later when ready
- Browse participating clinics without enrolling
- Book regular appointments without Healthier SG

### How much does Healthier SG cost?
Healthier SG enrollment is **free**. However:
- First consultation has standard clinic fees
- You receive $20 worth of Healthpoints after your first consultation (Singapore Citizens)
- Screenings and vaccinations have subsidized rates
- Standard healthcare consultation fees apply

### What is included in my Health Plan?
Your personalized Health Plan includes:
- **Health Assessment**: Comprehensive evaluation of your health status
- **Risk Factor Identification**: Assessment of potential health risks
- **Screening Schedule**: Age-appropriate health screenings
- **Vaccination Schedule**: Recommended vaccines
- **Lifestyle Recommendations**: Diet, exercise, and wellness advice
- **Follow-up Schedule**: Regular check-up recommendations
- **Health Goals**: Specific, measurable health improvement targets

### How often should I review my Health Plan?
**Recommended Schedule:**
- **Initial Review**: Within 3 months of enrollment
- **Annual Reviews**: Yearly health plan updates
- **As Needed**: When health status changes
- **Chronic Conditions**: More frequent monitoring as recommended by your doctor

### Can I change my selected doctor?
Yes, you can change your Healthier SG doctor:
1. Contact MOH or use HealthHub
2. Provide reason for change
3. Choose new participating doctor
4. Complete transfer process
5. Update your records with new doctor

### What if I move to a different area?
You can transfer to a doctor closer to your new location:
- Find participating doctors in your new area
- Request transfer through HealthHub
- Update your address and contact information
- Schedule new patient consultation with new doctor

### I'm not 40 yet. Can I still use the platform?
Yes! While Healthier SG is for ages 40+, our platform offers:
- General healthcare services for all ages
- Family doctor search and appointment booking
- Health information and education
- Preparation for Healthier SG eligibility

## Appointments & Bookings

### How far in advance can I book appointments?
Most clinics allow booking:
- **Standard appointments**: Up to 4 weeks in advance
- **Follow-up appointments**: Up to 2 weeks in advance  
- **Health screenings**: Up to 6 weeks in advance
- **Same-day appointments**: Available at participating clinics

### What's the difference between appointment types?
**General Consultation**: Regular check-ups, minor illnesses, prescription refills (15-30 minutes)
**Follow-up**: Review results, treatment adjustments, progress monitoring (15-20 minutes)
**Health Screening**: Comprehensive assessments, preventive care (45-60 minutes)
**Healthier SG Consultation**: Health plan development and reviews (30-45 minutes)
**Urgent Care**: Same-day treatment for non-life-threatening conditions (15-30 minutes)

### What should I do if I need to cancel my appointment?
**Online Cancellation:**
1. Log in to your account
2. Find the appointment
3. Click "Cancel Appointment"
4. Confirm cancellation

**Phone Cancellation:**
- Call the clinic directly
- Provide your confirmation number
- Cancel at least 2 hours before appointment

**Late Cancellation Policy:**
- Free cancellation up to 2 hours before
- No-show fees may apply for late cancellations
- Repeated no-shows may affect future booking privileges

### What if I'm running late?
**Call the clinic immediately** if you're running late. Most clinics:
- Allow 10-15 minutes of lateness
- May need to reschedule beyond this time
- Appreciate advance notice
- Can often accommodate small delays

### Can I book appointments for my family members?
Yes, you can book for family members if:
- They are added to your account as authorized users
- You have their consent
- You have their NRIC/FIN information
- You're authorized to make medical decisions for them (for minors/caregivers)

### What if I need an urgent appointment?
**For urgent but non-life-threatening issues:**
- Use "Urgent Care" booking option
- Check for same-day availability
- Call clinic directly for walk-in availability
- Use HealthHub after-hours nurse line

**For life-threatening emergencies:**
- Call 995 immediately
- Go to nearest hospital emergency department
- Don't wait for appointments

### How do I reschedule an appointment?
**Online Rescheduling:**
1. Log in to account
2. Find appointment
3. Click "Reschedule"
4. Select new date/time
5. Confirm changes

**Phone Rescheduling:**
- Call clinic directly
- Provide confirmation number
- Request new time slot

### What happens if I miss my appointment?
**No-Show Consequences:**
- Clinic may charge no-show fee
- Future booking privileges may be affected
- Healthier SG benefits may be impacted for missed consultations
- Doctor relationship may be affected

**If you miss an appointment:**
- Call clinic immediately to explain
- Reschedule for next available slot
- Be honest about reasons for absence
- Consider setting multiple reminders

### How do I know if my appointment is confirmed?
**Confirmation Methods:**
- SMS confirmation with appointment details
- Email confirmation with clinic directions
- In-app confirmation in your appointment history
- Calendar invite for your personal calendar

**If you don't receive confirmation:**
- Check your spam/junk folder
- Verify your contact information is correct
- Try booking again or call clinic directly
- Contact customer support if problems persist

## Insurance & Payments

### What insurance plans are accepted?
**Government Insurance:**
- **MediSave**: CPF Medical Savings Account (accepted at most clinics)
- **MediShield Life**: Basic medical insurance (automatic coverage)
- **CHAS**: Community Health Assist Scheme (subsidized care)

**Private Insurance:**
- Most major Singapore insurance companies
- Corporate insurance plans
- International health insurance
- Direct billing options with selected clinics

### How do I use my MediSave?
**MediSave Payment Process:**
1. Confirm clinic accepts MediSave
2. Verify your account details
3. Use NRIC/FIN for identification
4. Sign authorization for payment
5. Receive transaction receipt
6. Check remaining MediSave balance

**MediSave Limits:**
- Daily limit for outpatient treatments
- Annual limit for account holders
- Can combine with cash payments
- Automatic withdrawal from CPF account

### How do I know what's covered by CHAS?
**CHAS Coverage:**
- Consultations at participating clinics
- Common ailments treatment
- Selected chronic disease management
- Health screenings (age-appropriate)
- Vaccinations

**Checking Coverage:**
- Look for "CHAS Accepted" on clinic profiles
- Call clinic to confirm specific service coverage
- Check CHAS card for subsidy amounts
- Verify eligibility before appointment

### What are the typical consultation fees?
**Fee Ranges (before subsidies):**
- General consultation: $25-$50
- Follow-up consultation: $20-$40
- Health screening: $80-$200
- Healthier SG consultation: $30-$60
- Specialist referral: $50-$100+

**Factors Affecting Fees:**
- Doctor's experience and qualifications
- Clinic location and facilities
- Type and complexity of consultation
- Duration of appointment
- Additional tests or procedures

### Can I pay with cash or card?
**Payment Methods Accepted:**
- Cash (at most clinics)
- Credit cards (Visa, Mastercard)
- Debit cards
- PayNow / NETS
- Mobile payment (PayLah!, GrabPay)

**Payment Process:**
1. Pay at clinic reception after consultation
2. Present insurance cards first
3. Receive itemized receipt
4. Submit insurance claims if applicable
5. Keep receipts for tax/medical deductions

### How do I submit insurance claims?
**For Direct Billing Clinics:**
1. Present insurance card at appointment
2. Clinic submits claim directly to insurer
3. You pay only co-payment (if any)
4. Receive explanation of benefits
5. No additional claim submission needed

**For Manual Claims:**
1. Pay full consultation fee
2. Receive detailed receipt
3. Submit claim to insurance company
4. Include supporting documents
5. Receive reimbursement after review

### What if my insurance claim is rejected?
**Common Reasons for Rejection:**
- Service not covered under your plan
- Insufficient documentation
- Claims submitted after deadline
- Pre-existing condition exclusions
- Maximum benefit limits reached

**Appeals Process:**
1. Review rejection reason carefully
2. Gather additional documentation
3. Contact insurance company for clarification
4. Resubmit claim with additional information
5. Appeal through insurance company's formal process

**Getting Help:**
- Clinic staff can help with claim submissions
- Insurance company customer service
- Your employer benefits administrator
- Professional medical billing services

## Emergency Situations

### What constitutes a medical emergency?
**Life-Threatening Emergencies (Call 995):**
- Chest pain or pressure
- Severe difficulty breathing
- Loss of consciousness
- Severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis)
- Severe burns or trauma
- Stroke symptoms (FAST assessment)
- Severe bleeding
- Suspected heart attack

**Stroke FAST Assessment:**
- **F**ace: Facial drooping or uneven smile
- **A**rms: Arm weakness or numbness  
- **S**peech: Slurred speech or difficulty speaking
- **T**ime: Call 995 immediately

### What if I need healthcare outside clinic hours?
**After-Hours Options:**
- **Emergency Department**: Go to nearest hospital
- **24-Hour Clinics**: Check our platform for participating clinics
- **Urgent Care Centers**: Selected clinics offer extended hours
- **HealthHub Nurse Line**: 24/7 nurse consultation
- **TeleConsult**: Virtual consultation with on-call doctors

**Finding After-Hours Care:**
1. Search "24-hour" or "urgent care" on our platform
2. Call your clinic's after-hours number
3. Use HealthHub after-hours nurse line
4. Check hospital emergency departments
5. Call 1800 EMERGENCY for guidance

### What information should I have ready for emergency services?
**Essential Information:**
